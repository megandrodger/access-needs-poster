Designing for users on the
autistic spectrum

Autism spectrum disorders are developmental disorders that affect social and communication skills. As autism is wide-ranging, individual preferences can also vary widely across the spectrum so always test your designs to ensure you get the right balance.

Do use simple colours
Don't use bright contrasting colours

This is due to hyper/hypo sensitivity to colours of some users on the spectrum. It’s best to test with users to get the right balance.

Do write in plain language
Don't use figures of speech and idioms

Some users will take figurative language literally because of the careful attention to the way language is structured.

People on the autistic spectrum may experience over-sensitivity to colours. It's advisable to usemuted colours with lower saturation and hues derived from nature e.g. off-whites, mauves, olive greens, blue-greys and burnt oranges.
